New-hire time-to-productivity is weeks longer than it needs to be because onboarding is stitched together from a scattered set of tools — slide decks, shared drives, video libraries, and manager-led sessions. Managers improvise. Content goes stale. New hires show up to their first real task without a coherent path, and the org pays for the missed weeks in revenue ramp and early attrition.
