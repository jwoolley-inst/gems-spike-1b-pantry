tags: [recipe, blog]
pattern_name: "Blog Post"
composition:
  - {type: messaging_pillar, count: 1, required: true, notes: "anchor thesis"}
  - {type: pain_point, count: "1-2", required: true, notes: "reader's entry point"}
  - {type: proof_point, count: "1-2", required: true, notes: "what backs the claim"}
  - {type: statistic, count: "0-2", required: false, notes: "optional reinforcement"}
  - {type: customer_quote, count: "0-1", required: false, notes: "human texture"}
  - {type: brand_principle, count: "1-2", required: true, notes: "voice anchor"}
  - {type: tone_rule, count: "2-4", required: true, notes: "hard filters on language"}
parameters: [segment, product, angle, audience, tone]
output_guidance: "400–600 words. Hook in the opening sentence — a specific observation, not a claim. One or two subheads maximum. Close with a concrete next step, not a marketing CTA. No exclamation points. No 'leverage,' no 'synergy.'"
applies_to: [blog]
---

Build a blog post by pulling one messaging pillar as the anchor, one or two pain points that match the audience persona, one or two proof points that back the pillar, and (optionally) a statistic or customer quote. Every sentence runs through the tone_rule filters before it ships. Lead with a specific observation — a teacher name, a district outcome, an enrollment number. The reader should recognize themselves in the first paragraph. Trim anything that reads like a brochure. If the draft needs more than one subhead, split it into two posts.
