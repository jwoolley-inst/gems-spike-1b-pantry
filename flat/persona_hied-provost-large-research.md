The Provost owns academic strategy across colleges and schools. Their calendar is consumed by accreditation, faculty governance, retention committees, and budget defense. They do not touch the LMS day-to-day, but they will veto the LMS decision if it touches faculty workload, academic integrity, or student outcomes. They listen for "evidence," "at-risk early-warning," and "faculty will actually use it." They distrust vendor promises about adoption and want named reference calls with peer institutions.
